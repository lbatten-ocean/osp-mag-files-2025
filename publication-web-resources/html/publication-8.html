By Canada’s ocean supercluster team

Five years ago, Canada’s Ocean Supercluster (OSC) launched our first Innovation Ecosystem project: the Ocean Startup Proj-ect (OSP). It was a cold day in February in a packed room at the Emera Innovation Exchange, where ecosystem leaders would learn more about how this new, collaborative innovation eco-system project would bring the region, and in time, the country together around the opportuni-ty to help make Canada the best place to start and grow an ocean company.

This marked the first time that re-gional incubators, accelerators and ecosystem builders would come together to focus on the ocean sector specifically. As a country with the largest coast-line in the world, vast natural re-sources, and some of the most innovative people on the planet, this collaboration has focused on creating the much needed pipeline of new ocean compa-nies to support the transforma-tional potential for economic growth – something we have since defined as a 5X growth po-tential to $220-billion through Ambition 2035.

The idea was to give entrepre-neurs the mentorship, resources, and access to networks they

need to turn innovative ideas into real companies and also consider ocean applications for their ideas when they may not have otherwise. Through idea challenge programs, matchmak-ing and networking events, and opportunities to pitch and show-case ideas on national stages, the Ocean Startup Project would become a national launchpad for ocean entrepreneurs, culti-vating founders and companies that are helping shape the fu-ture of Canada’s ocean economy.

We know that Canada has histor-ically fallen below the global av-erage in terms of GDP contribu-tion from the ocean economy. At a time when there is exciting mo-mentum behind what it means to be a part of the ocean sector, the role of AI and emerging technologies as critical enablers,

While these numbers are impres-sive, the ecosystem benefits and outcomes are more than numer-ical value; they demonstrate that the model works as it was intend-ed - and that impact is clear.

Just a few years after being an-nounced, Startup Genome issued a global report that recognized OSP as one of North America’s top accelerators, highlighting its strong commercial outcomes and role in elevating Canada’s in-novation ecosystem ranking. Two years later, OSP’s ability to identi-fy, nurture, and accelerate start-ups continues to raise Canada’s reputation on the global ocean innovation stage.

For Canada’s Ocean Supercluster, one of the clearest measures

of success is seeing OSP-sup-ported companies graduate into leading OSC projects. Com-panies from across the coun-try such as OnDeck Fisheries AI, Hum.AI, CORSphere, SeaHawk Ro-botics, Glas Ocean Electric, and Scient, who first came through the Ocean Startup Project have since moved on to lead major projects through Canada’s Ocean Supercluster, achieving further follow-on investment, oppor-tunities in global markets, and further growth and scale-up. Notably, OSP challenge winners have gone on to raise $19-million in private investment to date. Their journeys demonstrate how the collective startup pipeline building approach works best: growing companies from con-cept pitching to industry leaders advancing large-scale projects. Programs such as the Ocean Startup Challenge and Creative Destruction Lab (CDL), as well as working in lockstep with OSC pro-gramming have been central to OSP’s approach.

Five years in, the collaboration

between OSC and OSP continues to deepen. Partnership on events like OceanFest showcase start-ups, create new connections, and highlight the growing talent pool in Canada’s ocean sector. While these programs support in-dividual companies, they are also building the broader ecosystem by strengthening networks, sup-porting workforce development, and raising the visibility of Can-ada’s ocean innovation on the global stage.

As we move forward toward Ambition 2035, the work of the Ocean Startup Project continues to be an important driver in Can-ada’s Ocean Supercluster’s strat-egy to grow a digital, sustainable, inclusive, and globally competi-tive ocean economy. As we cele-brate OSP’s fifth anniversary, we recognize its foundational role in helping make Canada the best place to start and grow an ocean company–one that identifies bold entrepreneurs, supports their early growth, and positions them to scale within Canada’s world-class ocean ecosystem.
