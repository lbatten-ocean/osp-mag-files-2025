Underwater video is famously unhelpful: colours wash out, detail disappears, and critical calls get delayed. Subseaify wants to fix that.

Led by Memorial University re-searcher Ali Ebrahimi, the start-up is building AI that restores true colour and fine detail from murky subsea footage — and then goes further, combining AI enhancement with sonar to help operators see further and even through obstacles like algae blooms. This capability is espe-cially valuable for naval intelli-gence and underwater monitor-ing scenarios, where clear and reliable visibility is crucial for identifying risks and gathering intelligence. Subseaify is chas-ing operational certainty across

domains: welds you can assess, anomalies you can trust, evi-dence you can hand to clients, regulators, or defense opera-tors without caveats.

“Most subsea video feels like

looking at the ocean through a foggy mask. We fuse phys-ics-based models with modern AI to bring back true colour and detail — so inspectors and sci-entists can act with confidence, not guesswork,” said Ali Ebrahi-mi, Subseaify Founder.

The company’s progression through the Ocean Startup Pro-ject (OSP) continuum shows how research becomes a real product. Subseaify first pres-sure-tested its assumptions in Lab2Market Oceans, where eight intensive weeks of customer discovery and mentor scruti-ny moved the team from bet-ter footage to better decisions.
