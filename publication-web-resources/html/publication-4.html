Planetary moved an ambitious idea into measurable climate impact in June 2025, delivering the first independently verified carbon removal credits from Ocean Alkalinity Enhancement (OAE). Working with Isometric and 350Solutions, the company documented 625.6 tonnes of CO₂ removed at a project site in Halifax, Nova Scotia—credits purchased by Stripe, Shopify, and British Airways. Recognized earlier as the Oceanshot winner in the 2020 Ocean Startup Chal-lenge, Planetary’s progress re-flects the kind of founder-led, science-first momentum the Ocean Startup Project set out to champion.

At its core, OAE restores and accelerates a natu-ral geochemical process that has helped regu-late Earth’s climate for millions of years. By adding finely ground,

naturally alkaline minerals to coastal waters, the approach reduces ocean acidity and sets off a chain reaction: as excess carbonic acid is neutralized, the ocean draws down more atmos-pheric CO₂ to rebalance. That carbon is then stored long term as stable bicarbonates—on the order of tens of thousands of years. Until Planetary’s 2025 issuance, no verified credits from OAE had been recognized. The milestone moved the field from pilots to documented, re-al-world climate impact, with transparent measurement and independent verification.

The credits were issued under Isometric’s industry-first OAE protocol, a rigorous framework for monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) that blends high-resolution modeling with real-world data to quantify net removal. The Halifax work cen-tered on the Tufts Cove coastal site, where Planetary doses alka-linity through an existing power plant outfall under government permits and strict environmen-tal oversight. To generate the credits, Planetary dosed 1,103.2 tonnes of alkalinity sourced from rigorously tested indus-trial byproducts; operated with Canadian regulatory oversight, environmental safeguards, and frequent monitoring; engaged scientists, stakehold-ers, and local

Planetary Co-Founder and CEO Mike Kelland captured the in-flection point succinctly: “This is a huge moment not just for Planetary, but for the entire field of carbon removal. We’ve moved from idea to impact. These cred-its are proof that OAE can be done safely, can be measured rigorously, and are part of solv-ing climate change at scale. It’s exciting to see science, com-munity, and innovation come to-gether in such a powerful way.”

The company frames this achievement as a beginning rather than an endpoint. As demand grows for durable, high-quality carbon removal, OAE is emerging as a critical com-plement to other approaches. Planetary’s process integrates with existing coastal infrastruc-ture, requires little land or elec-

tricity, and offers co-benefits such as reducing local ocean acidification. The path forward focuses on scaling with caution and care: advancing models, incorporating feedback, and working closely with regulators, researchers, and coastal com-munities.
